Part 2: Short Answer Questions
Q1. Two sandstones look very similar in grain size and color, but one is a quartz sandstone and the other is an arkose. What specific properties would you look for to distinguish them, and what do these differences tell you about how far each rock traveled from its source?
Q2. A geologist finds a rock layer that transitions from coarse conglomerate at the base to fine shale at the top, all within a single exposure. Using what you know about grain size, sorting, and depositional environments, propose an interpretation for how this sequence might have formed. What does the fining-upward pattern suggest?
Q3. You are examining a limestone sample. Describe the step-by-step process (the ID decision tree) you would follow to determine the specific type of limestone (e.g., micritic, oolitic, fossiliferous, crystalline, coquina, chalk). What observations and tests would you perform at each step?
Q4. A rock layer contains mudcracks, evaporite minerals, and thin beds of shale with occasional layers of poorly-sorted conglomerate. What depositional environment best explains this combination of features? Justify your answer by explaining what each individual feature tells you about the conditions during deposition.
Q5. Explain why chemical weathering and physical (mechanical) weathering are described as working together. How does the surface area concept help explain their interaction? Give a specific example from the lecture that illustrates this relationship.
